# The Lamp District asset bible

**Scope:** RODOH Gate 4 production dignity floor for Program 004 and the Underworld representation.

## Visual classification

The Lamp District is an inhabited civic underworld maintaining the exterior classification of a sterile Meridian ruin. Its visual system must hold two readings at once: ordinary neighborhood life illuminated by carefully rationed lamps, and defensive infrastructure arranged to make that life appear absent to an outside observer.

## Material grammar

- **Base:** coal-black service stone, oxidized plum metal, salt-grey conduit, soot-dark glass.
- **Civic light:** rationed amber, used for people, schools, clinics, routes, and actionable controls.
- **Recorded inheritance:** muted green, used only where the engine says a consequence has become part of the run.
- **Breach:** iron orange, used for `wake`, `breached`, `exposed`, and immediate visibility risk.
- **Text:** bone and warm grey rather than white, preserving long-duration low-light readability.

The environment must read as maintained infrastructure rather than a natural cave. Repeated arches, pipes, pressure doors, reservoirs, civic signage, and stacked rooms show a city living inside inherited machinery.

All assets are original local vectors. No external image, font, stock, or generative-media dependency is carried into the repository.

## Role appearances

Every authored role receives a distinct 16×16 portrait and body appearance:

- Wakekeeper
- Surface Bearer
- Maintainer
- Interlocutor
- Witness
- Deliberator
- Sovereign Exception

Scale, silhouette, tool, and lamp placement must distinguish roles at gameplay size. Color alone is insufficient.

## Movement motifs

Each of the eight canonical movements owns a motif. The motif depicts the governing object or relation, rather than a generic dungeon icon: school lamp, reservoir charter, drainage liturgy, sleeping market, war lattice, surface household, heat commons, and public depth map.

## Representation contract

Lamp District material treatment applies across Board, Map, Hall, Encounter, Aperture, Globe, Underworld, portraits, and cartridge chrome. Underworld is the grammar-specific surface. The shared surfaces remain faithful alternate representations and may not substitute their own fiction or state.

## Accessibility

- Interactive targets retain the World mobile target floor.
- Amber, green, and breach states also carry text and structural markers.
- Forced-colors mode removes decorative imagery and preserves hierarchy through system colors and borders.
- Reduced-motion mode disables transitions and animation.
- The seven-layer map collapses to a linear reading order on narrow screens.
- SVG assets include titles and descriptions.
