# Book IV World staging: The Lineage Commons

**Status:** post-RODOH-1.0 product staging only. This document reserves the receiver, presentation, asset, accessibility, and browser-acceptance boundary for *The Godscar Codex, Book IV: The Lineage Commons*. It adds no runtime code, source-plane inspection, Program number, cartridge, theme, or asset binding.

**Arc dependency:** `BigBirdReturns/axm-arc#152` and draft staging PR `BigBirdReturns/axm-arc#153`.

**Release dependency:** World Gate 8 must close before any Book IV implementation enters the active product train.

## Product classification

Book IV is a continuity-governance and inherited-future game. The player manages a Lineage Commons: carriers, developmental environments, transmission meshes, succession relations, obligations, alternatives, refusal, future standing, and the political institutions that authorize continuity work.

World's future responsibility is faithful projection. Arc must remain authoritative for source validation, Assembly legality, state transition, deterministic resolution, exact receipts, and portable custody. World may present evidence, claimants, carriers, alternatives, Assembly stages, state tracks, and inherited consequences. It may not decide personhood, invent future claimants, manufacture consent, or infer a lawful continuity arrangement from prose.

## Candidate representation

The grammar-specific surface is provisionally named **Lineage Commons**. It should support four coordinated views rather than one oversized dashboard.

### Commons view

A civic and material map showing:

- carrier bodies and substrates;
- developmental bands and transition points;
- habitat and route dependencies;
- transmission meshes and archive custody;
- succession forums and recognized relations;
- obligation and compatibility-debt concentrations;
- present, absent, delayed, and future claimants;
- visible alternatives and single points of capture.

### Assembly view

A bounded procedure showing:

- endangered function and decision horizon;
- evidence tier, provenance, intervention history, limits, dissent, and uncertainty;
- current claimants, carriers, custodians, developmental advocates, succession representatives, and Future Chamber;
- need, consent, custody, refusal, alternatives, future standing, review, and termination;
- authorized burden, reversible elements, review date, and exact receipt.

The Assembly view must make absence legible. A missing voice cannot become an empty card that the receiver silently interprets.

### Continuity view

A topology showing how bodies, habitats, archives, routes, institutions, and successor relations perform the endangered function. This view should expose which workaround or custodian has become indispensable and which replacement paths are real.

### Inheritance view

A before-and-after ledger showing:

- all eight state tracks;
- changed carriers, habitats, archives, routes, succession rules, constituencies, and alternatives;
- obligations, dissent, uncertainty, review triggers, and termination conditions;
- futures newly possible and newly impossible;
- connected receipts inherited by Books I, II, or III.

## Eight-track presentation

The receiver must present these tracks without reducing them to one health bar:

1. Carrier Viability
2. Developmental Integrity
3. Archive Trust
4. Succession Legitimacy
5. Alternative Capacity
6. Refusal Capacity
7. Visibility
8. Compatibility Debt

Protective tracks run from crisis at 0 toward durable plural capacity at 4. Compatibility Debt requires explicit inverse language because a higher value narrows future choice. Color alone may not communicate direction or crisis.

## Interaction boundary

A complete player journey should be:

```text
inspect the endangered function and decision horizon
→ read evidence, provenance, and absent voices
→ map carriers, development, transmission, succession, obligations, and alternatives
→ convene, defer, refuse, or bypass an Assembly
→ allocate resources, custody, burden, and temporary authority
→ enter the Arc-authored intervention
→ receive the exact result and state transitions
→ record dissent, uncertainty, review, termination, and foreclosed futures
→ export the changed run
→ resume in a fresh holder context
```

World may allow the player to select authored options, claimants, allocations, and parties. Every feasibility verdict and state-changing consequence remains Arc-owned.

## Neutral-first requirement

Book IV must begin with a neutral receiver before a canonical cartridge receives bespoke expression. The neutral surface must:

- render unfamiliar claimant, carrier, developmental, archive, succession, and obligation vocabulary directly from source;
- support several people sharing one carrier profile and one person spanning several carriers;
- show a delayed or future claimant without inventing a conventional present-day avatar;
- preserve unknown namespaced memory;
- avoid borrowing First Charter, Waking Tower, Ilyon, Lamp District, or Relief Circuit art;
- remain usable with typography, structure, and neutral marks alone;
- refuse malformed Book IV source without receiver-authored repair.

## Production asset program

Book IV requires a larger semantic asset plan than a single lead portrait because its subject is a network of bodies, habitats, archives, and possible futures. The production floor should still remain finite.

### Grammar-level assets

These belong to the reusable Book IV receiver rather than one campaign:

- **Lineage Commons topology atlas:** carriers, developmental bands, transmission, succession, obligations, alternatives, and future standing.
- **Nine-pressure atlas:** threatened function, scarcity, carrier field, developmental environment, transmission path, succession rule, custody claimant, excluded claimant, and approaching trigger.
- **Eight-track atlas:** one distinct structural mark for every state track, including an inverse Compatibility Debt mark.
- **Assembly procedure strip:** need, consent, custody, refusal, alternatives, future standing, review, and termination.
- **Claimant and carrier fallback library:** neutral appearances for present people, distributed persons, ecologies, machines, archives, habitats, collectives, delayed claimants, and Future Chamber representation.
- **Continuity-capture diagram:** a legible progression showing alternatives, refusal, provenance, and review disappearing from necessary dependency.

### Canonical-cartridge assets

After Arc selects the reference campaign, the cartridge should receive:

- one runtime-loaded hero environment;
- one Assembly environment or civic forum;
- one continuity topology or cross-section;
- one lead present claimant portrait;
- one lead carrier or custodian portrait;
- one future/delayed claimant representation that does not pretend to be an ordinary present person;
- campaign-beat motifs;
- faction, evidence, obligation, and consequence marks;
- project-owned provenance and responsive/accessibility receipts.

The asset pack must depict mechanism. Generic infants, family trees, DNA helices, or fertility symbolism are insufficient because they collapse biological reproduction, machine succession, ecological continuity, archive reconstruction, and political inheritance into one human-normal image.

## Candidate visual grammar

The reusable grammar should distinguish four material domains:

- **carrier:** bodies, substrates, habitats, route organisms, and machines;
- **development:** nurseries, schools, symbiont succession, training, maturation, and category change;
- **transmission:** archives, language, ritual, skill, maintenance, memory, and provenance;
- **succession:** descendants, forks, resurrection, adoption, divergence, shared inheritance, and ending.

Visual hierarchy should show dependency and alternatives rather than coding factions as good or evil. Continuity Capture should appear through narrowing paths, single-owner custody, missing receipts, or irreversible thresholds.

## Accessibility requirements

Book IV's content makes accessibility part of the subject rather than an optional presentation feature.

The receiver and asset pack must provide:

- keyboard operation through every Assembly stage;
- linear reading order for topology and Assembly on narrow screens;
- structural labels in addition to color and shape;
- long descriptions for dense topology, Assembly, and continuity-capture diagrams;
- forced-colors treatment that retains relation and direction;
- reduced-motion treatment for developmental and succession transitions;
- target-size acceptance for claimant and alternative selection;
- text alternatives that distinguish a person, carrier, habitat, archive, institution, and future claim;
- explicit announcement of track direction, especially Compatibility Debt;
- no timed interaction whose duration assumes one cognitive or motor tempo.

## Sound and sensory direction

Sound remains procedural and local-first. A future Book IV cue family should distinguish:

- claim opened;
- Assembly convened;
- claimant absent or delayed;
- alternative established;
- refusal preserved;
- continuity captured;
- review due;
- succession recognized.

No sound cue may carry exclusive semantic information. Book IV should not inherit the neutral fallback tone as its finished sonic identity.

## Browser acceptance

One exact World candidate must pass desktop and mobile journeys that:

- import a valid Book IV cartridge through the ordinary file path;
- inspect all four views;
- demonstrate lawful and refused Assembly paths;
- preserve several people sharing a carrier and one person spanning several carriers;
- change all eight state tracks through Arc receipts;
- preserve dissent, uncertainty, obligations, review, termination, and future claims;
- complete the canonical campaign;
- export, clear holder context, import, and resume exactly;
- preserve connected operation and unknown namespaced memory;
- refuse malformed source;
- operate offline with no external asset or runtime request;
- pass keyboard, forced-colors, high-contrast, reduced-motion, target-size, and long-description checks.
