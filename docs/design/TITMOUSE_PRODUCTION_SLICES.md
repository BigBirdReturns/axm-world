# Titmouse production slices

“Titmouse” is the production-direction bar for the finished reference game. It
does not mean adding ambient motion to every screen. It means composing a small
number of authored beats so character, choice, consequence, and durable record
read as one scene.

## Runtime law

- Animation follows an authoritative state transition; it never predicts or
  fabricates one.
- Anticipation, commit, impact, consequence, and aftermath are distinct beats.
- Cartridge presentation may supply faces, poses, motifs, copy, and sound. The
  runtime owns timing only after the engine proves what happened.
- Reduced motion collapses time, not information. Muted play loses nothing.
- The static final frame must still answer: what changed, who changed, what was
  recorded, and what happens next.
- Imported cartridges receive a neutral fallback. Presentation never changes
  Arc law or authored identity.

## Slice 1 — First Charter entry

The cold path is one directed chain:

1. The held cartridge plaque lifts from the bay.
2. The runtime opens into the inhabited Hall, not an abstract board behind a
   modal.
3. Maren and the starting squad are visible while the founding oath is read.
4. Selecting an oath commits immediately through `resolveDramaCard`.
5. The response is derived from actual before/after deltas and remains until
   acknowledged.
6. Maren presents The Cellar through the existing Hall contract dialogue on
   desktop; the governed one-panel phone flow lands on the same Cellar staging
   sheet directly.
7. Reload/resume restores the chosen doctrine and changed organization without
   replaying the entry or oath.

### Acceptance

- Desktop and mobile reach the oath with no explanatory handoff.
- Hall, Maren, squad, oath, and all authored choices remain legible.
- The response cannot appear before the engine commits the choice.
- The response is compact at first glance; exact deltas remain inspectable.
- Continue reveals The Cellar as the next action through the existing engine
  path: Maren's Hall dialogue on desktop, Cellar staging on phone.
- The transition is skippable, nonblocking, and immediate under reduced motion.
- No new network, schema, engine, or save-version dependency is introduced.
- Resume does not replay session-only staging.

the operator must independently describe the conflict, choice, consequence,
