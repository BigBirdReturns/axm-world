# The Relief Circuit World acceptance contract

**Status:** Gate 6 preparation on top of the Gate 4 Lamp District branch. This document and the read-only receiver model do not bundle The Relief Circuit, assign it a Program number, ship a vessel-management interface, or claim browser acceptance.

**Dependencies:**

1. Gate 4 must merge The Lamp District Underworld receiver and four-cartridge regression baseline.
2. Gate 5 must accept one exact Arc head containing the canonical Relief Circuit source, compiled cartridge, authoring product, complete campaign, and connected Lamp District transition.
3. World must vendor that exact Arc head before the Gate 6 product candidate is qualified.

## Receiver authority

World may render:

- the creator-authored Common Ship source;
- engine-authored challenge availability and completion;
- engine-owned ship-state values and exact state-change receipts;
- Arc composition feasibility, rejection reasons, dependencies, and single points of failure;
- holder-owned roster selections, representation choices, and run memory;
- the connected Lamp District source and state carried by the accepted transition.

World may not:

- compile `common-ship-pocket/1`;
- infer or recompute the six Common Watch tests;
- apply ship-state effects;
- invent a lawful roster, handoff, precedent, or cross-cartridge consequence;
- replace an invalid source with receiver defaults;
- treat a body profile as an occupation or a decorative species modifier.

The preparation module under `src/world/common-ship/` follows the existing Underworld pattern. It inspects the registered source plane and derives a display model from source, Arc-owned node status, and engine-owned cartridge state. It deliberately contains no composition evaluator or resolver.

## Required management surfaces

The Gate 6 player experience must provide one coherent vessel-management representation with access to the following views.

### Mission and decision horizon

The holder can inspect the physical, informational, institutional, and manufactured urgency before committing a watch. The interface distinguishes the external deadline from the life-fraction, recovery, and continuity cost imposed on each profile.

### Body and role profiles

Embodiment profiles expose scale, occupied volume, minimum passage, reach, locomotion, environment, sensorium, direct and mediated interfaces, temporal structure, and lineage dependencies. Role requirements remain separate. The player can see both why a person is qualified and what conditions make that qualification usable.

### Watch composition

The player selects actual people, not abstract lineage bonuses. The interface presents the exact Arc verdict for:

1. role coverage;
2. temporal overlap;
3. habitat compatibility;
4. translation resilience;
5. handoff continuity;
6. life-fraction fairness.

An unlawful watch remains inspectable and names every failed condition. A lawful watch names the absent actor, excluded body, dependency, reserve path, and single points of failure.

### Common systems allocation

The player allocates habitat bands, translation paths, direct interfaces, standby, stores, and emergency authority. Allocation changes must be authored by the cartridge and resolved by Arc. World presents the consequences and does not maintain a parallel ship economy.

### Ship state

The eight tracks are displayed continuously:

- Habitat Integrity
- Temporal Coherence
- Translation Trust
- Roster Resilience
- Stores and Care
- Continuity
- Visibility
- Compatibility Debt

Every change links to the exact report receipt that caused it. Initial values, current values, clamps, and authored reasons remain distinguishable.

### Handoff and precedent

After resolution, the player records dissent, injury, readiness debt, promises, missing persons, and uncertainty. The resulting precedent states what is newly possible, newly impossible, newly governable, and inherited as infrastructure. A handoff is a political event, not a transition screen.

### Connected Lamp District operation

The accepted run reaches the Lamp District through the Gate 5 transition object. The player conducts a relief and descent operation using the actual watch, people, stores, dependencies, and ship state brought to the Tomb. The return must preserve changed ship and Tomb facts in one exportable run record.

## Representation plan

The Common Ship receiver should introduce one principal vessel-management representation while reusing the neutral shell, encounter, cartridge object, and custody surfaces.

The principal representation needs:

- a vessel cross-section or systems map;
- habitat-band occupancy and continuity paths;
- overlapping clocks and decision horizon;
- roster and reserve composition;
- translation provenance and intermediary dependencies;
- direct versus mediated interface access;
- the eight ship-state tracks;
- visible handoff and precedent history;
- a docking or route relation to the Lamp District.

The representation must remain usable at desktop and mobile sizes and under keyboard-only, forced-colors, high-contrast, reduced-motion, and large-target conditions.

## Production dignity target

The Relief Circuit requires:

- cartridge-owned cast appearances for every founding profile;
- motifs for the six Common Watch tests, seven ship systems, eight state tracks, and handoff or precedent;
- one runtime-loaded vessel environment;
- one lead portrait;
- one vessel cross-section or environmental-depth layer;
- original-authorship provenance;
- neutral fallback for unknown Common Ship cartridges.

The receiver must not branch on `relief-circuit` to understand Common Ship law. Cartridge-specific art and Program metadata may be keyed by exact computed cartridge identity; the management grammar must be source-plane generic.

## Gate 6 browser acceptance

One exact World candidate must complete the frozen journey on desktop and mobile:

> Inspect the mission and decision horizon, read body and role profiles, compose a lawful watch, identify the excluded actor and dependency, allocate habitat and translation systems, resolve the pressure, perform a handoff, establish precedent, reach the Lamp District, conduct a connected relief and descent operation, return with changed ship and Tomb state, export the run, load it in a fresh holder context, and resume exactly.

Permanent acceptance also requires:

- exact Arc provenance and drift verification;
- complete World tests and production build;
- regression journeys for First Charter, Waking Tower, Ilyon, and Lamp District;
- exact `axm-cartridge-run/v3` export, fresh-context import, and resume;
- persistence-write failure recovery;
- offline boot;
- neutral imported Common Ship presentation;
- accessibility and production-asset receipts.

## Prepared boundary

This preparation branch establishes the receiver data model and acceptance contract only. It intentionally does not add Program 005, a bundled cartridge, a Common Ship scene, a roster UI, state controls, cross-cartridge execution, assets, or browser tests. Those changes become valid only after the exact Gate 5 Arc candidate exists.
