3. World vendors that exact Arc head and enforces strict drift verification before qualification.

## Receiver authority

World may render:

- the creator-authored Common Ship source;
- engine-authored challenge availability and completion;
- engine-owned ship-state values and exact state-change receipts;
- Arc composition feasibility, rejection reasons, dependencies, and single points of failure;
- holder-owned roster selections, representation choices, and run memory;
- the connected Lamp District source and state carried by the accepted transition.

World may not:

- compile `common-ship-pocket/1`;
- infer or recompute the six Common Watch tests;
- apply ship-state effects;
- invent a lawful roster, handoff, precedent, or cross-cartridge consequence;
- replace an invalid source with receiver defaults;
- treat a body profile as an occupation or a decorative species modifier.

The module under `src/world/common-ship/` follows the existing Underworld pattern. It inspects the registered source plane and derives a display model from source, Arc-owned node status, and engine-owned cartridge state. It deliberately contains no composition evaluator or resolver.

## Required management surfaces

The Gate 6 player experience provides one coherent vessel-management representation with access to the following views.

### Mission and decision horizon

The holder can inspect the physical, informational, institutional, and manufactured urgency before committing a watch. The interface distinguishes the external deadline from the life-fraction, recovery, and continuity cost imposed on each profile.

### Body and role profiles

Embodiment profiles expose scale, occupied volume, minimum passage, reach, locomotion, environment, sensorium, direct and mediated interfaces, temporal structure, and lineage dependencies. Role requirements remain separate. The player can see both why a person is qualified and what conditions make that qualification usable.

### Watch composition

The player selects actual people, not abstract lineage bonuses. The interface presents the exact Arc verdict for:

1. role coverage;
2. temporal overlap;
3. habitat compatibility;
4. translation resilience;
5. handoff continuity;
6. life-fraction fairness.

An unlawful watch remains inspectable and names every failed condition. A lawful watch names the absent actor, excluded body, dependency, reserve path, and single points of failure.

### Common systems allocation

The player allocates habitat bands, translation paths, direct interfaces, standby, stores, and emergency authority. Allocation changes must be authored by the cartridge and resolved by Arc. World presents the consequences and does not maintain a parallel ship economy.

### Ship state

The eight tracks are displayed continuously:

- Habitat Integrity
- Temporal Coherence
- Translation Trust
- Roster Resilience
- Stores and Care
- Continuity
- Visibility
- Compatibility Debt

Every change links to the exact report receipt that caused it. Initial values, current values, clamps, and authored reasons remain distinguishable.

### Handoff and precedent

After resolution, the player records dissent, injury, readiness debt, promises, missing persons, and uncertainty. The resulting precedent states what is newly possible, newly impossible, newly governable, and inherited as infrastructure. A handoff is a political event, not a transition screen.

### Connected Lamp District operation

The accepted run reaches the Lamp District through the Gate 5 transition object. The player conducts a relief and descent operation using the actual watch, people, stores, dependencies, and ship state brought to the Tomb. The return must preserve changed ship and Tomb facts in one exportable run record.
