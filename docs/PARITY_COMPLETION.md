# Bundled product parity — inventory, sequence, completion contract

## Current-scope definition

Parity means the shipped local-first browser product gives every bundled cartridge the same guarantees:

1. canonical Arc law and deterministic full-campaign reachability;
2. cold entry, opening decision, first action, consequence, ledger, exact reload and run export;
3. Board, Map, Hall, Encounter, Aperture, and Globe access on desktop and mobile;
4. cartridge-owned role appearances, challenge motifs, all-surface material identity, and distinct procedural globe palette;
5. EN / zh-Hant runtime chrome, keyboard-visible focus, reduced motion, local procedural sound controls, forced-color and high-contrast fallbacks;
6. neutral white-label fallback for unknown/imported cartridges.

## Gap inventory and build sequence

| Sequence | Gap | Closure |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | First Charter and The Waking Tower reused neutral Rodoh role bodies | Cartridge-owned portrait/body packs for every authored role |
| 2 | Theme identity concentrated on Board | Map/Hall/Encounter/Aperture/Globe treatments and per-cartridge globe palettes |
| 3 | Ilyon receiver journey was shallower than First Charter | Shared three-cartridge browser parity journey plus full deterministic campaign/custody harness |
| 4 | Sensory/accessibility controls were inconsistent | Persistent sound/reduced-motion controls and CSS fallbacks |
| 5 | Parity could regress silently | Focused CI workflow and governed asset/campaign tests |

## Completion decision

The local-first parity gate passes only when Arc and World normal suites, builds, exact vendoring, full-campaign custody tests, and desktop/mobile bundled journeys pass on reviewed heads. No cartridge may ship third-party player-facing fiction or fall back to another cartridge's art.

## Deferred products, not hidden release gates

Multiplayer/netcode, cloud identity/sync, marketplace economics, publisher signing, cinematic video, and high-resolution painted illustration are separate products. They are not required to call the shipped local-first cartridge/runtime scope complete.
